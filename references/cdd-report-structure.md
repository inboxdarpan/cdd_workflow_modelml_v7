# Commercial Due Diligence Report Structure

When writing the final Commercial Due Diligence report, ensure it covers the following sections comprehensively:

## 1. Executive Summary
- High-level overview of the company and its core value proposition
- Key findings from the due diligence process
- Overall confidence rating and investment thesis

## 2. Company & Product Overview
- Founding team and leadership background
- Core product offerings and technical capabilities
- Target customer segments and use cases

## 3. Market Size & Growth
- Total Addressable Market (TAM) analysis
- Market growth drivers and trends
- Key sub-segments and their respective sizes

## 4. Competitive Landscape
- Direct and indirect competitors
- Competitive positioning (strengths, weaknesses, differentiators)
- Market share and momentum

## 5. Business Model & Financials
- Pricing strategy and revenue model
- Funding history and key investors
- Notable customer wins and partnerships

## 6. Strategic Priorities & Opportunities
- Potential areas for expansion or product development
- Go-to-market (GTM) strategy improvements
- Strategic partnerships or acquisitions

## 7. Key Risks & Mitigations
- Market and competitive risks
- Execution and operational risks
- Regulatory or compliance risks

Ensure all data points are supported by inline citations and include a comprehensive references section at the end.
